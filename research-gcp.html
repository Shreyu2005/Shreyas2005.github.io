GCP Planning & GDOP-Based Accuracy Optimization

Undergraduate research focused on reducing field effort while maintaining mapping accuracy by optimizing Ground Control Point (GCP) placement/quantity using GDOP-based modeling and ArcGIS-ready outputs.

← Back to Research Overview

Goal

Reduce survey workload while keeping accuracy high

  • Optimize how many GCPs are needed and where they should be placed.
  • Use GDOP-style modeling to predict measurement geometry quality and expected accuracy.
  • Deliver outputs that teams can use directly in GIS tools for planning and field execution.

Approach

Modeling + optimization + GIS deliverables

  • Built a predictive workflow to evaluate candidate GCP layouts and their expected impact on accuracy.
  • Integrated terrain/mission constraints to keep recommended placements realistic.
  • Generated ArcGIS-compatible deliverables: KML, shapefiles, and feature layers.

Outcome

Clear, field-usable recommendations

  • Provided an optimization-based method for selecting efficient GCP placements.
  • Helped reduce required field effort while protecting accuracy targets.
  • Structured the results for easy usage in mapping/survey workflows.
